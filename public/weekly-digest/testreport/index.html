Executive Summary#

  • 1-3 bullets summarising the main answer to the key question.
  • Written for a busy reader who may only read this section.
  • Clearly state scope, time frame and focuse.

Key Judgements#

  1. KJ1 First key judgement in one sentence, with scome and time frame plus probability indicator.

  2. KJ2

  3. KJ3

Context#

Briefly describe:

  • Who is involved
  • What the issue is
  • Why it matters
  • The time frame
  • Definitions or assumptions.

Evidence and Open Sources#

Summarise the main sources used, by type:

  • Official public documents
  • Media reporting (described by theme, not outlet if preferred)
  • Public datasets
  • Academic / NGO / Think tank analysis
  • Other OSINT (imagery, social media etc.)

Analysis#

Explain how the evidence leads to the key judgements.

  • Describe mechanisms, drivers and constraints
  • Discuss alternative explainations where relevant
  • Highlight areas of uncertainty and gaps in reporting
  • Be explicit: what is inference, what is directly observed

Implications#

Describe why this matters, for whom and in what time frame.

  • Implications for Australia and the Indo-Pacific etc.
  • Policy, economic or security implications
  • Possible second and third order effects

Outlook#

Provided a bounded outlook in time:

  • Assessed developments
  • Key uncertainties
  • Indicators that would strengthen or weaken assessment

Method Notes#

Briefly note:

  • Any structured analytical techniquest used (trend analysis, competing hypothesis etc.)
  • Key limitations in data
  • Any important caveats for interpretation.
